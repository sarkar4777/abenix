KINGDOM OF SAUDI ARABIA — NATIONAL TOURISM STRATEGY 2030
=========================================================

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 positions tourism as a key pillar
of economic diversification, targeting 150 million annual visits by 2030 and
a tourism sector contribution of 10% to GDP (up from 3.5% in 2023).

The National Tourism Strategy outlines five strategic pillars:

1. RELIGIOUS TOURISM EXCELLENCE
   - Hajj capacity expansion to 30 million pilgrims annually by 2030
   - Year-round Umrah facilitation with streamlined e-visa processing
   - Investment: SAR 250 billion in Holy Cities infrastructure
   - Target: 50 million religious visitors annually

2. LEISURE & ENTERTAINMENT DESTINATIONS
   - Giga-projects: NEOM, The Red Sea, AMAALA, Qiddiya, Diriyah Gate
   - Seasonal festivals: Riyadh Season, Jeddah Season, AlUla Moments
   - Target: 70 million leisure visitors annually
   - Hotel capacity expansion: 500,000 new rooms by 2030

3. CULTURAL & HERITAGE TOURISM
   - UNESCO World Heritage Sites: Al-Ula (Hegra), At-Turaif (Diriyah),
     Historic Jeddah, Al-Ahsa Oasis, Hima Cultural Area
   - New museum network: National Museum expansion, regional museums
   - Investment: SAR 50 billion in cultural infrastructure
   - Target: 15 million cultural tourists annually

4. BUSINESS & MICE TOURISM
   - Riyadh as a global business hub (F1, boxing, esports, conferences)
   - New convention centers: Riyadh Expo 2030 facilities
   - Target: 10 million business visitors annually
   - Investment: SAR 80 billion in MICE infrastructure

5. ECO-TOURISM & ADVENTURE
   - Asir National Park development
   - Red Sea coral reef preservation and diving tourism
   - Mountain tourism in Tabuk and Asir regions
   - Desert safari experiences in the Empty Quarter
   - Target: 5 million eco-tourists annually

INVESTMENT PIPELINE
-------------------
Total committed investment: SAR 1.2 trillion (US$320 billion)

Major projects:
- NEOM (The Line, Trojena, Oxagon): SAR 1,875 billion — mixed-use mega city
- The Red Sea Project: SAR 28 billion — luxury coastal resort
- AMAALA: SAR 12 billion — ultra-luxury wellness tourism
- Qiddiya: SAR 30 billion — entertainment city near Riyadh
- Diriyah Gate: SAR 75 billion — cultural destination
- AlUla Development: SAR 50 billion — heritage tourism

REGIONAL DEVELOPMENT PRIORITIES
-------------------------------
1. Riyadh: Business hub, entertainment capital (Riyadh Season, Qiddiya, F1)
2. Makkah: Religious tourism excellence, capacity expansion, crowd management
3. Madinah: Spiritual tourism, cultural heritage, green city development
4. Jeddah: Coastal leisure, Red Sea gateway, historic district revival
5. Eastern Province: Industrial tourism, Ithra cultural center, island resorts
6. NEOM: Future city, luxury adventure, ski resort (Trojena), marine (Sindalah)
7. Al-Ula: UNESCO heritage, luxury desert resorts, cultural experiences
8. Asir: Mountain tourism, eco-tourism, summer escape destination
9. Tabuk: Archaeological sites, Red Sea coast, adventure tourism

VISA & ACCESS STRATEGY
-----------------------
- E-visa expansion to 75+ countries (from 49 in 2023)
- Visa-on-arrival for GCC residents of any nationality
- Transit visa (96-hour stopover program)
- Special event visas (F1, Hajj, Umrah, Saudi Seasons)
- Target: reduce visa processing to under 5 minutes by 2026

SUSTAINABILITY COMMITMENT
--------------------------
- 50% renewable energy for tourism facilities by 2030
- Zero-waste resorts (Red Sea Project, AMAALA)
- Coral reef restoration program (100,000 coral fragments)
- Carbon-neutral tourism operations in NEOM
- Biodiversity preservation: Arabian leopard, marine reserves

This strategy document is issued by the Ministry of Tourism,
Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, under the guidance of Vision 2030.
